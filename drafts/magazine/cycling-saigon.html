Cycling Saigon

Five days navigating Vietnam's most electric city on two wheels.

Cycling · Asia · 5 days

The first thing you notice cycling in Ho Chi Minh City is not the heat, not the noise, and not even the sheer density of motorbikes — it's the flow. What looks, from the outside, like absolute traffic anarchy turns out to have its own deep logic: a living, breathing organism that somehow accommodates ten million people and several million vehicles in a state of improbable, exhilarating harmony.

Our guide, Linh, has cycled these streets every day for fifteen years. She glides through intersections without hesitation, turning back occasionally to check we're still behind her, amused by the deer-in-headlights expression that never quite leaves our faces in those first hours.

District by District

We structure our days around districts. District 1 — the historic centre — reveals itself over the first morning: the grand colonial façade of the Post Office, the pink Renaissance extravagance of Notre-Dame Cathedral, the narrow French-era streets around Dong Khoi where tailor shops and bánh mì carts share the pavement with air-conditioned boutiques.

Ben Thanh Market

Day two begins at 6am outside Ben Thanh Market, where the produce section is at its best in the early morning light. Linh buys us bánh cuốn — steamed rice rolls with pork, mushroom, and fried shallot — from a stall that has been here longer than anyone can remember. We eat standing, bikes propped against a pillar, the market beginning to hum around us.

By mid-morning we're in District 4, a residential neighbourhood on the south bank of the Ben Nghe Channel that tourists rarely reach, its lanes too narrow for coach tours but perfect for bicycles. The alleyways here smell of jasmine and fish sauce, and every corner offers something to see.

The Mekong Day Trip

On day four we load the bikes into a boat and head south into the Mekong Delta. The delta is a different Vietnam: slower, greener, and draped in the easy rhythms of agricultural life. We cycle between floating markets, sample tropical fruit straight from the orchards, and watch the sun descend over the rice paddies in a display of golds and pinks that feels almost theatrical in its generosity.

Practical Notes

Best time to go

December–April (dry season). Average temperature 28–35°C. Cycling is sweaty year-round, but the dry months spare you the afternoon monsoon.

Getting there

Fly into Tan Son Nhat International Airport (SGN). Most major Asian hubs connect well. The city centre is 30 minutes by taxi.

Fitness level

★★ (2/5). Terrain is flat. The challenge is traffic confidence, not physical exertion. Beginners welcome.

Bikes

XO Tours (District 3) offers quality road and hybrid bikes by the day or week. Helmet and lock included. Guided tours depart 7am daily.

Cost

Budget USD 40–60/day for accommodation, food, and bike hire. Vietnam is excellent value; eating street food keeps costs very low.

Safety

Traffic appears chaotic but is slow-moving in the city. Maintain a steady pace, avoid sudden stops, and trust the flow. Wear a helmet.
